Abstract

Current state-of-the-art (SOTA) video diffusion models rely heavily on semantic attention, which fails at fine-grained physical causality. When prompted to generate a teacher writing on a whiteboard, the model exhibits a severe spatial-temporal disconnect: the teacher's hand points or gestures randomly, while the text hallucinates onto the board independently.

For foundational literacy (NIPUN Bharat), where a child must observe exact stroke order and hand-eye coordination, this lack of causal grounding renders the generated video unusable. In this project, we introduce the Imagination Engine, which moves from semantic approximation to mathematical, pixel-level physical constraints by decoupling generation into two distinct, mathematically controlled subsystems orchestrated by an Action Graph.

Our Approach: Decoupling Motion and Rendering

Within the "Imagination Engine," we are solving the causality gap by splitting the generation into two distinct subsystems, orchestrated by a high-level Action Graph to ensure logical sequencing. We synthesize techniques from isolated fields of AI research to build this unified architecture.

Subsystem A
Trajectory-Guided Motion

Instead of relying on text prompts for motion, we feed dense optical flow paths (the exact $x,y$ coordinate sequence of the letter strokes) directly into the diffusion U-Net. This forces the hand's pixel cluster to physically trace the stroke order.

Powered by: Multiscale Fusion (DragNUWA) + Object Motion Control Modules (MotionCtrl)

Subsystem B
Glyph-Conditioned Gen

Standard models treat text as a texture, causing alien glyphs. We integrate auxiliary OCR-based latent modules to generate perfect, legible typography frame-by-frame, ensuring the ink appears exactly where the hand dictates.

Powered by: Glyph-Control & OCR-embeddings (AnyText, AnyText2, TextDiffuser)
